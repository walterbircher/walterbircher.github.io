What Is EDMD

Emery-Dreifuss muscular dystrophy (EDMD) is a rare, progressive muscle disease. It was first described in the 1960s. EDMD is known for three common feature groups: joint stiffness, muscle weakness, and heart problems.

1. Joint stiffness

Joint tightness often starts early, especially in elbows, heels, and neck/back.

2. Muscle weakness

Weakness often begins in upper arms and lower legs and can slowly progress.

3. Heart involvement

Heart rhythm and conduction problems can be serious and may need urgent treatment.

Genetics

EDMD can be inherited in X-linked, autosomal dominant, or autosomal recessive patterns. The most common genes discussed on current source sites are EMD and LMNA.

X-linked

Often linked to EMD variants. Usually affects males more directly, with female carriers at some risk.

Autosomal dominant

Often linked to LMNA variants. One altered copy can be enough to cause disease.

Autosomal recessive

Two altered copies are needed. Parents may be carriers without obvious symptoms.

Diagnosis and care

Diagnosis

Diagnosis usually combines symptoms, exam findings, heart testing, and genetic testing.

Heart follow-up

Ask how often ECG, Holter, and echocardiogram are needed for your situation.

Daily management

Discuss physical therapy, mobility supports, and emergency planning with your specialists.
